My Story


I am David Liang, and I write this sob story in hopes to gain favor with employers and admissions offices (LOL). I was born in GuangZhou, China on October 7th, 1999. I currently live in California, and just graduated with a Psychology and Cognitive Schience degree at UCSD.

I was born into a loving family that includes two hard-working parents, and a very smart and funny older brother. However, before my developing mind was able to even fathom the existence of my father, he immigrated to the United States in Janurary 2000 in search of better opportunities. This left my mother alone to take care of both me and my brother. Eventually in 2008, My family was able to join my father in the States.Despite being in America for almost 10 years, my dad did not know how to speak English, and you've probably heard it all before - both of my parents ended up working low-paying laborous jobs in order to put food on the table. Being a child of strict Chinese parents, I also felt the same kind of pressure my parents felt. I was constantly reminded of how inadequate I am at school, being bullied almost everyday. I was also reminded of the financial hardships of the family every night at the dinner table, with my dad constantly scolding me to get good grades and get into a good college so we wouldn't have to live in my aunt's garage anymore. It was thanks to all those hardships that I was able to overcome adversity and become who I am today.
