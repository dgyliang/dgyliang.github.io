Final Project for UCSD's COGS 108 course, an upper division practical data science course.

This project uses python statistical libraries such as Matplotlib, Seaborn, Pandas, etc to perform analysis on several potential mediating factors in predicting obesity rates in U.S states, including family income, fastfood restaurant accessibility, and education level. Regression models were used to analyze relationships of these factors, and geospatial maps were created for visualization of data.
We found that household income has the strongest correlation to obesity rate per capita, however the study itself is heavily flawed, in part due to our limitation of access to updated census data, as well as the nature of the study being strictly correlational, with no account for possible extraneous factors.

The Github repository is unfortunately private, due to their wishes to remain anonymous.
